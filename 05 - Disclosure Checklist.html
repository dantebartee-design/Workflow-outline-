How to use: work top-to-bottom. The process boxes (naming, folders, sign-off) are non-negotiable. The substantive items are prompts — final judgement on legal sufficiency always rests with the lawyer.
1 Intake & setup
  • Matter is open in Clio; correct client & matter confirmed.
  • Disclosure type and deadline identified and noted.
  • All source documents gathered into the working space.
  • Working from the approved template / playbook.
2 Naming & filing
  • Named to convention: YYYYMMDD description (initials)
  • Date is first; description is plain lowercase.
  • Initials reflect who has touched it, e.g. (D) / (D L).
  • Saved in the correct folder for its stage.
  • One file — no duplicate "final_v3" versions.
3 Content & accuracy
  • All required sections present and complete.
  • Party names, dates & references correct throughout.
  • Figures & facts verified against the source.
  • Cross-references and exhibit labels match the text.
  • No placeholder text, brackets, or "TBC" left in.
  • Read end-to-end at least once.
4 Exhibits & attachments
  • Every exhibit referenced is actually attached.
  • Stored in 📁 Disclosures / Attachments.
  • Each exhibit labelled and dated correctly.
  • No stray, duplicate, or privileged docs included.
5 Review & revision trail
  • A short note recorded for each significant change.
  • Initials appended (not replaced) after your edits.
  • Outstanding questions flagged or resolved.
  • Any handoff back to the other person is noted.
6 Approval & filing
  • Lawyer review complete; final approval recorded in the filename.
  • No tracked changes or comments left in the final.
  • Moved to 📁 Done — locked, no further edits.
  • Final copy exported to Clio (system of record).
  • Filed / served by the deadline; confirmation saved to the matter.
